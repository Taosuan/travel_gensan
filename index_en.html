Day 1: Arrival and a Surprise Reunion

On December 23, 2024, I visited General Santos, located in the southern part of the Philippines, for the very first time. The purpose of this trip was to meet my girlfriend’s parents and spend Christmas with her family.

After a one-hour domestic flight from Cebu, I landed at the airport and was greeted by the warm tropical humidity and radiant sunshine unique to this region. A friend of hers picked us up by car, and we enjoyed a 30-minute drive to the city center. Along the way, the expansive blue skies and lush greenery welcomed me to this new place.

As soon as we arrived, we headed to a restaurant in a shopping mall. The visit was a surprise for her parents, and we had planned to reveal it during the meal. Witnessing the emotional reunion between her and her parents after three years, with tearful hugs and joy, was deeply touching even for me. Over dinner, we enjoyed traditional Filipino dishes like kare-kare and sinigang, sharing laughter and conversation throughout. Afterward, we spent the night at a cottage on Susan’s Beach with her family and friends.

Day 2: A Day at Susan’s Beach

The next morning, I woke up to the breathtaking sight of a pristine blue sea and a beautiful sandy beach right in front of me. Susan’s Beach is known for its crystal-clear waters, where you can even spot small fish and starfish along the shoreline. Strolling along the sandy beach and feeling the gentle sea breeze instantly relaxed me. Later, we took a boat ride offshore and experienced diving for the first time. The underwater world, filled with vibrant coral reefs and colorful tropical fish, felt like stepping into another realm. The sight of vivid schools of fish swimming right in front of me left an unforgettable impression.

After enjoying the sea, we headed to her family’s home. Unlike Japanese houses, their home had a charming, distinctly Filipino design. That evening, we gathered for a special Christmas dinner, where I tasted lechon, a roasted whole pig, for the first time. The crispy skin and juicy meat were exceptionally delicious, adding to the festive atmosphere. After dinner, we went up to the rooftop to watch fireworks and gaze at the star-filled sky, creating memories I’ll treasure forever.

Day 3: Natural Pools and a Night Market

On Christmas Day, we visited Olaer Swimming Resort, known for its natural pools filled with crystal-clear, refreshing water. Surrounded by greenery and rocks, the serene environment felt like an extension of nature itself. Jumping into the cool water was invigorating and refreshing for both body and soul. That evening, we explored Paskong Pamilya, a bustling night market. The market was filled with local food stalls and attractions, including shooting games and a thrilling swing ride, which brought out the child in me. A photo booth allowed us to capture the moment with family and friends. Among the many treats, the fresh mango smoothie stood out the most. Its perfect balance of sweetness and tartness embodied the essence of the tropical Philippines. The lively atmosphere of the market and the warmth of the local people made it an unforgettable night.

Day 4: A Day Trip to Davao

The following day, we went on a road trip to Davao. Our first stop was the Crocodile Park, where I enjoyed seeing a variety of animals native to the region.

On our way back, we stopped by Camp Sambros to try their zipline. The exhilarating ride through the open air, surrounded by lush greenery, was thrilling and offered a unique way to experience the natural beauty of the area.

Day 5: Shopping and a Night by the Beach

On the final full day, we visited KK Shopping Mall in General Santos City to shop for souvenirs. Among the unique items, I tried a dish called Takuyaki, a takoyaki-inspired snack without octopus, which was an interesting twist for someone from Japan. In the evening, we headed to White Haven Beach Resort to enjoy a relaxing night. With a cold beer in hand, we played games, danced, and shared laughter with family and friends by the shore. The vast, star-filled sky and the soothing sound of the waves provided a tranquil yet magical backdrop to the festivities.

Day 6: A Heartfelt Goodbye

On the final morning, we enjoyed an early swim in the sea before taking family photos on the beach.

On our way back to General Santos, we stopped at Jollibee for a farewell lunch. After packing at her home, they drove me to the airport. It was hard to say goodbye, but I left with a promise to return. The six days I spent in General Santos were filled with warmth, family connections, and the beauty of the tropical environment. I look forward to visiting again in 2025.
